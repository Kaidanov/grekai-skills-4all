# AI displacement stress test — modeling the job you might not keep

The viral prompts treat the salary as a stable base you optionally build on top of. For a growing
set of roles that assumption is shaky: the income you're "planning around" may shrink, change, or end
on a timeline you don't control. This file does two jobs — score the **day job's** exposure, and
re-rank the **side-income paths** by how AI-durable they are. The goal isn't doom; it's to convert a
fragile "optional side hustle" into deliberate **resilience** when exposure is real.

## Part 1 — Score the day job's exposure

You're not predicting the user's exact fate; you're sizing a risk so the plan can absorb it. Assess:

- **Task automatability.** What fraction of the role is routine, digital, language/data-based, and
  already doable by current tools (drafting, summarizing, basic coding, first-line support, routine
  analysis, translation, scheduling, standard document work)? The higher that fraction, the higher the
  exposure.
- **Judgment / accountability / trust content.** Work requiring contextual judgment, being the person
  legally or relationally *accountable*, managing humans, or high-trust relationships is more durable.
- **Physical / regulated / in-person component.** Hands-on, licensed, or physical-presence work is
  far less exposed in the near term.
- **Bottleneck position.** Even in an automatable field, the person who *directs, verifies, and owns*
  the AI output is more durable than the person doing the now-automatable task.
- **Employer & sector signals.** Hiring freezes, "efficiency"/restructuring language, AI-tooling
  mandates, or a shrinking team are concrete signals — weight them over vibes.

Translate to a **horizon, not a verdict**: roughly, is this role's income at material risk within
~2 years, ~2–5 years, or longer? Then carry that horizon into the plan: **if exposure is high, the
exit/resilience build is no longer optional, and the runway and timeline must assume the salary may
not last the full plan.**

State this as risk, with humility — these timelines are genuinely uncertain, predictions in this area
have a poor track record in both directions, and the user knows their job better than any model does.
Offer the assessment as a planning input to pressure-test, not a prophecy.

## Part 2 — Re-rank the side-income paths by AI durability

Apply the same lens to the income paths from engine step 4, and re-rank them.

**Down-weight (AI is rapidly commoditizing the core value):**
- Generic copywriting, basic content production, simple translation.
- Template-level graphic/web design, boilerplate code, basic data entry/cleanup.
- Anything whose pitch is "I'll produce the artifact" when a tool now produces a passable artifact
  for near-free. The artifact's price is falling toward zero.

**Up-weight (value AI doesn't capture, or that AI makes you *better* at):**
- **Judgment & taste** — choosing what's good, what to make, what to cut.
- **Trust, accountability & relationships** — being the person a client relies on and can hold
  responsible; local presence; reputation.
- **Distribution & audience** — attention is the scarce asset; owning a channel is durable.
- **Physical / in-person / regulated** delivery.
- **Integration & orchestration** — assembling AI tools into an outcome a client can't or won't
  assemble themselves; selling the *result*, not the keystrokes.
- **Domain depth** in a specific industry where context, data access, and consequences matter.

**The reframe to give the user:** the durable move is usually to **use AI as leverage to sell a
human-anchored outcome** (judgment, trust, distribution, accountability), not to compete with AI on
producing cheap artifacts. A side income built on "I do the thing AI now does for free" is building on
sand; one built on "I own the relationship/result and use AI to deliver it faster" is building on rock.

## Part 3 — Run the contingency

Pressure-test the consolidated plan against involuntary loss:

- **"50% / zero in N months."** If the day-job income halved, or stopped in N months (N = the horizon
  from Part 1), does the household stay above its localized floor runway? If not, the fix is
  **floor-first**: enlarge the emergency fund and protect essentials *before* any growth bets.
- **Skill-durability hedge.** Steer at least part of the build toward the up-weighted, AI-durable
  categories — so the resilience plan isn't itself exposed to the same wave threatening the job.
- **Speed vs. safety.** Higher day-job exposure argues for moving sooner on resilience — but never by
  forfeiting the floor or breaching a hard gate in `blind-spots.md`. Faster, not reckless.
